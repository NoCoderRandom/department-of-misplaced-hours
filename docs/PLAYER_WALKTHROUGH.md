# Player Walkthrough

This is a simple spoiler walkthrough for finishing **The Department of Misplaced Hours** with a mouse.

Basic controls:

- Move the mouse around the picture. When the cursor becomes a hand, click to inspect or use that object.
- Click inventory items along the bottom to select them, then click something in the room to use the item there.
- Right-click or press `Escape` to put an item away or close ordinary panels.
- Use `Map`, `Notes`, and `Hint` if you forget what the game is pointing at.

## Short Walkthrough

1. Start a new shift and press `Clock In`.

   Thinking: the game begins by making you accept the weird office orientation. This gives you the `Visitor Badge`, which is your first authority item.

2. In Reception, take the `Blank Form 11-H` from the In-Tray and the `Rubber Stamp` from the desk.

   Thinking: this is classic point-and-click logic. A blank official form plus an official stamp should create permission.

3. Use the `Rubber Stamp` with the `Blank Form 11-H` to make the `Stamped Form`.

   Thinking: the door needs official paperwork, so the form has to be made official first.

4. Optional but useful: inspect the Future Phone.

   Thinking: the phone gives the vending-machine clue in sound form. It is not needed immediately, but remember that it gives three groups of clicks.

5. Select the `Stamped Form`, then click the Circle Door to enter Clock Hall.

   Thinking: the Circle Door is checking for stamped permission, not your badge.

6. In Clock Hall, read the Personnel Calendar. Go back to Reception and read the Desk Memo if you have not already.

   Thinking: the Mood Clocks puzzle needs two written clues. The memo gives the first and last moods. The calendar explains the middle order.

7. Solve Mood Clocks with:

   `Regret`, `Hunger`, `Calm`, `Joy`

   Thinking: Regret knocks first. Hunger comes before Calm. Joy signs last.

8. Go to Security Office. Inspect at least one evidence source: Monitor Bank, Incident Board, or Security Log.

   Thinking: the office will not issue serious authority unless you first inspect evidence.

9. Use the `Visitor Badge` or `Stamped Form` on the Key Cabinet, then choose `Take Key`.

   Thinking: the cabinet accepts either identity or paperwork authority and gives you the `Security Key`.

10. Use the `Security Key` on the Evidence Safe, then choose `Take Warrant`.

   Thinking: the key opens the safe, but only after you have seen evidence. This gives you the `Audit Warrant`.

11. Go to Interrogation Booth. Inspect the Rain Window and keep or leave the note.

   Thinking: the rain is the visual version of the phone clue: seven, three, one. Keeping the note only adds a reminder to inventory.

12. Inspect the Interview File.

   Thinking: this explains the missing hour and why the vending machine matters.

13. Go to Records Archive. Inspect the Archive Table.

   Thinking: the table maps symbols to categories:
   `Triangle = Apology`, `Circle = Appetite`, `Eye = Witness`, `Square = Rest`.

14. Take the `Time Token` from the Coin Drawer.

   Thinking: the vending machine needs a strange coin, and this is it.

15. Go to Break Room. Inspect the Cork Board and take a `Paper Cup`.

   Thinking: the Cork Board gives the category order. The cup is needed because the vending machine dispenses the missing hour into something.

16. Return to Records Archive and solve Index Drawers with:

   `Triangle`, `Circle`, `Eye`, `Square`

   Thinking: the Cork Board order is Apology, Appetite, Witness, Rest. Convert those categories back into symbols using the Archive Table.

   Alternate solution: if you have inspected Security evidence and have the `Audit Warrant`, you can select the warrant and use it on the Index Drawers instead.

17. Open the Glass Case and choose `Take Them`.

   Thinking: this gives you the personal records: `Misfiled Folder`, `Mirror Shard`, and `Your Missing-Person File`. Your file is important because it can prove who you are without needing the official warrant route.

18. Go to Break Room and use Memory Vending. Enter:

   `731`

   Thinking: this is the same clue from the phone/tape clicks or the Rain Window: seven, three, one.

19. Choose `Take Them` from Memory Vending.

   Thinking: you receive the `Cup of Missing Hour` and the `Server Fuse`. These are both final-room tools.

20. Go to Mirror Office.

   Thinking: the Mirror Office allows either official authority (`Audit Warrant`) or personal case-file authority (`Your Missing-Person File`), but you still need the glass-case records and vending reward first.

21. Use the `Mirror Shard` on the Black Mirror.

   Thinking: the mirror reveals the server sequence:
   `Circle`, `Triangle`, `Eye`, `Square`.

22. Use the `Server Fuse` on the Server Console.

   Thinking: the console needs power before it can accept instructions.

23. Use `Your Missing-Person File` or the `Audit Warrant` on the Red Intercom.

   Thinking: the intercom first needs identity. Your file proves personal identity; the warrant proves official administrative identity.

24. Use the `Cup of Missing Hour` on the Red Intercom.

   Thinking: after identity, the Auditor needs the missing hour itself.

25. Answer the Auditor:

   `The clerk holding the file.`

   `Seven-three-one.`

   `Outside the system.`

   Thinking: the answers come from the case-file story, the vending clue, and the cup warning.

26. Use the Server Console and enter:

   `Circle`, `Triangle`, `Eye`, `Square`

   Thinking: this is the order shown by the completed Black Mirror.

27. Choose an ending at the Exit Door:

   - Use `Your Missing-Person File` for the Filed Ending.
   - Use `Cup of Missing Hour` for the Escaped Ending.
   - Use `Audit Warrant` for the Audit Ending, if you have it.

   Thinking: the final choice is about what you trust most: being documented, escaping the ledger, or making the Department answer officially.

## If You Are Stuck

- Read `Notes`; it tracks the important clues you have seen.
- Use `Hint`; exact answers are hidden behind `Show Answer`.
- If an item is selected by mistake, right-click or press `Escape`.
- If a room refuses you, read the message carefully. It usually names what is missing.
